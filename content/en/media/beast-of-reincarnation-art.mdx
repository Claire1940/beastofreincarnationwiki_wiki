<Section icon={Shield} title="Mastering the Parry and FP Mechanics">

Parrying is the heartbeat of the **beast of reincarnation art** gameplay loop. Every successful parry negates damage entirely and provides a significant boost to your FP (Flow Power) gauge. Without mastering the parry, you cannot access the Bloom Arts that are required to defeat larger bosses and elite enemies.

The timing for parries is deliberate. You must observe the preparatory movements of your opponents. Large bosses often telegraph their heavy blows with specific wind-up animations. Deflecting these not only protects your health but also accelerates your transition into the tactical selection phase.

| Action | FP Gain | Risk Level | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Perfect Parry** | High | Medium | Zero damage, massive FP boost |
| **Standard Block** | Low | Low | Reduced damage, minimal FP |
| **Dodge/Evasion** | None | High | Repositioning, no FP gain |
| **Bloom Art Hit** | Variable | Low | Massive Stun gauge increase |

<Callout type="warning" title="Parry Timing">
Heavy blows from colossal bosses can still be deflected, but the timing window is much tighter than with standard enemies. If you miss the window, you will take significant chip damage or be knocked back.
</Callout>

Once the FP gauge in the lower right corner is glowing, you are ready to unleash a Bloom Art. This transition from high-speed defense to tactical offense is what sets this combat system apart.

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Bloom Arts and Tactical Selection">

When you activate a **beast of reincarnation art** (Bloom Art), time slows down significantly. This is not just a visual flair; it is a mechanical necessity. During this slow-motion window, you can observe the enemy's current frame of animation. If they are mid-charge, you might select a restraining Art. If they are recovering, a high-damage javelin might be better.

<Callout type="success" title="Strategy Tip">
Use "Binding Roots" specifically when a boss is charging a purple-glowing attack. This usually signifies a move that cannot be parried normally, and the Art is the only way to cancel the animation.

<Section icon={Target} title="Emma's Skill Tree and Progression">

While Koo provides the magical foundation through the **beast of reincarnation art** system, Emma provides the raw power. Her effectiveness is determined by the SP (Skill Points) you earn during your adventures. These points are spent in a comprehensive skill tree that evolves throughout the game.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Earn SP through Combat">
    Engage in battles and defeat bosses to accumulate Skill Points. Bosses provide the largest chunks of SP required for high-tier nodes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Unlock Follow-up Attacks">
    Focus on the "Synergy" branch of the tree first. This allows Emma to perform more complex follow-up attacks after Koo uses a Bloom Art.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Acquire Finishers">
    Unlock abilities like "Helm Splitter" to increase the damage dealt to enemies with a full Down Gauge.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Enhance Art Synergy">
    New Bloom Arts obtained from bosses (like Sakura: Javelin) often have dedicated nodes in Emma's tree to improve their combined effectiveness.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Skill Category | Primary Benefit | Recommended for |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Offense** | Increased raw damage | Fast clearing of trash mobs |
| **Synergy** | Better follow-up attacks | Boss battles and elite units |
| **Utility** | Reduced cooldowns/costs | Players who prefer frequent Art use |
| **Defense** | Improved block/recovery | Beginner players learning parry timing |

<Callout type="info" title="Skill Tree Expansion">
The skill tree is not static. Every time Emma absorbs the power of a defeated boss, a new branch of the tree awakens, offering techniques that were previously inaccessible.
</Callout>
