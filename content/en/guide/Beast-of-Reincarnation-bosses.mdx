<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Synergy: Emma and Ku">

The heart of the combat against **Beast of Reincarnation bosses** lies in the "deadly dynamic duo" system. Emma provides the raw melee power and defensive parries, while Ku offers tactical versatility. This relationship is governed by Fluorescent Points (FP), which function similarly to mana or magic points in traditional JRPGs. 

Emma's primary role is to engage in close-quarters combat with her katana or handle flying enemies with her wrist-mounted crossbow. However, her most important tool is the parry. Successfully timing a parry not only opens the boss to a finishing move but is the primary method for generating FP.

| Role | Primary Actions | Resource Interaction |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Emma** | Sword combos, parries, crossbow, traversal | Generates FP via parries |
| **Ku** | Automatic bites, tail whips, tactical abilities | Consumes FP for special moves |

Ku’s abilities are triggered by tapping a button to suspend the battlefield in slow motion. This allows you to choose from six different special abilities. These range from offensive fireballs to crowd-control roots that can bind a boss in place.

| Ku Ability | Effect | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fireball** | Ranged fire damage / HP recovery on hit | Sustaining Emma's health |
| **Earth Roots** | Shivers shields / Binds enemies | Breaking armored boss guards |
| **Tail Whip** | Area of effect (AoE) knockback | Managing adds during boss fights |

<Callout type="tip" title="Master the QTEs">
Whenever Ku deploys an attack, a Quick Time Event (QTE) appears on the screen. Nailing these events replenishes a substantial chunk of Emma's health bar, reducing the reliance on healing items.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Boss Breakdown: The Mutant Stag">

The first major hurdle among the **Beast of Reincarnation bosses** is the Mutant Stag of the Oguchi Buffer Zone. This creature is a "lumbering giant" with extraordinary senses, capable of punishing players who rely solely on aggression. The fight is divided into distinct phases where the stag utilizes its antlers for sweeping melee strikes and its flora-infused body to launch projectiles.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Preparation and Scouting">
    Locate the campfire overlooking the lakeside lair. Scavenge the nearby strongbox for chainmail armor and ensure your crossbow is fully loaded.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Parry the Antler Sweeps">
    The Stag's primary attack is a wide-reaching antler swipe. Focus on timing your parries to build FP quickly. The parry window is generous, so stay calm.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Deploy Ku's Binding Roots">
    Once you have enough FP, use Ku's root ability to tangle the Stag's ankles. This binds the beast to the spot, allowing Emma to land heavy katana strikes safely.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Ranged Transition">
    When the Stag retreats or fires spikes from afar, switch to the long-range bow or wrist crossbow. Aim for the glowing blighted spots on its hide.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Attack Pattern | Type | Counter Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Antler Swipe** | Melee / AoE | Perfect parry to build FP |
| **Deadly Spikes** | Ranged / Projectile | Dodge or block with terrain |
| **Lumbering Charge** | Linear / High Damage | Side-step and follow with Ku's fireball |
| **Blight Pulse** | AoE Burst | Move out of the immediate radius |

<Callout type="warning" title="Boss Retreat">
Defeating the Mutant Stag in the first encounter may not be the end. Bosses in this world are resilient and may retreat to their lairs, requiring you to track them down for a final confrontation later in the story.
</Callout>

</Section>

Success against **Beast of Reincarnation bosses** is often determined before the fight even begins. Using these tools to reach vantage points can allow you to initiate a boss fight with a stealth attack or a plunging strike.
