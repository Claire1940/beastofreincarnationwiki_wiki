<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Beast of Reincarnation DLSS & Upscaling Technologies">

To help players achieve stable framerates, the PC version of *Beast of Reincarnation* features robust support for modern upscaling technologies. **Beast of Reincarnation DLSS** support is integrated directly into the graphics menu from day one, allowing players with NVIDIA GeForce RTX graphics cards to leverage AI-driven super resolution.

Using DLSS is highly beneficial because the game's dense vegetation, complex plant-hair physics for Emma, and real-time blight transformations can heavily tax your GPU. 

### Supported Upscaling Technologies

| Technology | Supported Hardware | Key Feature | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **NVIDIA DLSS 3** | RTX 40-Series GPUs | Frame Generation & Super Resolution | Maxing out high refresh rate monitors |
| **NVIDIA DLSS 2** | RTX 20/30-Series GPUs | AI-powered image reconstruction | Balancing image quality and FPS |
| **AMD FSR 3** | Most modern GPUs | Spatial upscaling & Frame Generation | Non-RTX and handheld PC players |
| **Intel XeSS** | Intel Arc & major GPUs | Hardware/software reconstruction | Alternative upscaling option |

<Section icon={Swords} title="How to Optimize Graphics Settings for Maximum FPS">

Achieving the perfect balance between visual fidelity and high performance is essential for mastering the hybrid combat system. If you are experiencing frame drops during intense encounters with Nushi (the giant regional bosses), follow this step-by-step optimization guide.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Enable Hardware Accelerated GPU Scheduling">
    Before launching the game, ensure Windows is optimized. Go to Windows Settings > System > Display > Graphics. Click "Change default graphics settings" and turn on "Hardware-accelerated GPU scheduling." Restart your PC.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Activate DLSS or FSR">
    Open the in-game graphics menu. Set your Upscaling Method to **DLSS** (if using an RTX card) and choose the **Quality** preset. If you are targeting higher framerates on a 1440p or 4K monitor, switch to **Balanced**.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Adjust Heavy Environmental Settings">
    The blight effects and dense forests are graphically intensive. Lower **Shadow Quality** and **Foliage Density** from Ultra to Medium. This simple tweak can improve performance by up to 20% without sacrificing overall visual quality.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Configure Motion Blur and Depth of Field">
    Turn off **Motion Blur** to maintain visual clarity during rapid camera movements. Keep **Depth of Field** on if you prefer cinematic combat menus, or disable it to squeeze out a few extra frames.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Optimized Settings Reference Table

| Setting Option | Recommended Value | Performance Impact | Visual Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Display Mode** | Fullscreen | Low | None |
| **Upscaling Method** | DLSS (RTX) / FSR (Non-RTX) | High (Positive Boost) | Low (at Quality preset) |
| **Shadow Quality** | Medium | High | Medium |
| **Foliage Density** | Medium | Medium | Medium |
| **Effects Quality** | High | Medium | High |
| **Ray Tracing** | Off (unless using RTX 4070+) | Very High | High |

<Callout type="success" title="Handheld Optimization">
For players using Windows-based handheld devices like the ROG Ally or Lenovo Legion Go, setting the game to 1080p, turning on **AMD FSR 3** to Balanced, and utilizing Medium/Low presets will yield a stable 45-60 FPS.
</Callout>
