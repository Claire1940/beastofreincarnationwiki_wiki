<Section icon={Swords} title="Mastering Combat: Bloom Arts and Parrying">

The core of the **Beast of Reincarnation Japanese title**'s gameplay lies in its hybrid combat system. While the movement and basic strikes are fast-paced and real-time, the game introduces a "tactical pause" mechanic through Bloom Arts. When you activate a Bloom Art, time slows down significantly, allowing you to choose the most effective technique based on the enemy's current state.

Successful combat revolves around managing three primary gauges:

| Gauge Type | Function | Recovery Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Health Bar** | Represents total vitality. | Using healing items or specific Bloom Arts. |
| **Down Gauge** | Fills to create a "Final Takedown" opening. | Sustained damage and heavy attacks. |
| **FP Gauge** | Energy source for K's power and Bloom Arts. | Perfectly parrying enemy attacks. |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Perfect the Parry">
    Wait for the enemy's preparatory movement. Timing a parry perfectly negates all damage and rapidly fills your FP gauge.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Activate Bloom Art">
    Once FP is full, trigger the Bloom Art menu. Time will slow, giving you a moment to assess weaknesses or status effects.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Exploit Stun Gauges">
    Use specific skills like "Binding Roots" to restrain enemies. This fills the stun gauge, leading to a one-hit finish opportunity.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Team Finishers">
    Coordinate Emma's strikes with Koo's follow-up attacks, such as the "Helm Splitter," to maximize damage on boss targets.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Parry Timing">
Heavy blows from bosses can still be deflected, but the timing window is much tighter than standard enemy attacks. Missing a parry against a boss often results in massive health loss.
</Callout>

</Section>

The art direction of the **Beast of Reincarnation Japanese title** has drawn significant praise for its "Ghibli-esque" charm.
