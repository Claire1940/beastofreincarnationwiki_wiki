<Section icon={Swords} title="Directing the Combat: Emma and Koo Mechanics">

The **Beast of Reincarnation director** has implemented a combat loop that rewards precision and timing. Players control Emma directly, utilizing her katana for fast-paced strikes and her plant-like hair for vertical traversal and assassinations. However, the true depth lies in how Emma’s actions fuel Koo’s abilities. By performing "Just-Guard" parries, players earn points that can be spent on Koo’s command techniques.

When the player opens the command menu, the action slows down, allowing for tactical assessment. This "Command Menu" system is a signature directorial choice, ensuring that even the most intense boss fights have moments of strategic clarity. This prevents the game from becoming a pure "button-masher" and instead requires players to manage resources and position effectively.

<Callout type="warning" title="Combat Warning">
Failing to parry will leave Emma without the points needed to trigger Koo’s defensive skills, making high-level encounters significantly more difficult.
</Callout>

| Mechanic | Input / Trigger | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Katana Strike** | Real-time button press | Direct physical damage to malefacts |
| **Just-Guard Parry** | Timed defensive input | Blocks damage and generates Command Points |
| **Koo Technique** | Command Menu selection | Unleashes powerful support or offensive skills |
| **Plant Traversal** | Environment interaction | Reaches elevated positions for falling kills |

</Section>

<Section icon={Map} title="Managing the Blight: Environmental Direction">

The environmental direction of *Beast of Reincarnation* is centered on the concept of a "Living World." The Blight is not a static background; it is an active force that can transform a peaceful plain into a dense, hostile forest in a matter of moments. These transformations are often triggered by the presence of a "Nushi"—a giant malefact that rules a specific region.

Exploration is guided by Emma's unique physiology. Her vine-like hair allows her to cross collapsed bridges and scale ruined skyscrapers, blending post-apocalyptic urban decay with traditional Japanese nature. The **Beast of Reincarnation director** has stated that the world is designed to be explored vertically as much as horizontally, rewarding players who look for hidden routes and elevated vantage points.
