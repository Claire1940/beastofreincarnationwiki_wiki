<Section icon={Sparkles} title="PC Optimization & Graphics Settings">

Achieving a consistent 60 frames per second requires balancing visual fidelity with system performance. Several graphics options impact performance more than others, and tweaking these settings can yield massive frame rate gains without sacrificing the overall aesthetic of the game.

| Setting | Recommended Value | Performance Impact |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Shadow Quality | Medium | High |
| Volumetric Fog | Low or Medium | High |
| Ray Tracing | Off | Very High |
| Texture Quality | High (Requires 8GB+ VRAM) | Low |
| Anti-Aliasing | TAA or DLSS Quality | Medium |

Shadows and volumetric effects are the primary culprits behind frame rate drops in forested areas and dungeons. Lowering these options by a single tier can recover up to fifteen percent of your overall performance. If your graphics card supports hardware-accelerated upscaling, utilizing DLSS, FSR, or XeSS is highly recommended to boost frame rates at higher resolutions.

Adjusting the field of view (FOV) slider can also impact performance. A wider FOV forces the engine to render more objects on screen simultaneously, which can bottleneck performance on systems with weaker processors. Keep the FOV close to the default value if you experience CPU-bound stutters.

<Callout type="tip" title="Upscaling Tip">
When using DLSS or FSR, avoid the Ultra Performance mode unless you are playing at 4K resolution. At lower resolutions like 1080p, this mode can make the game look overly blurry and create shimmering artifacts around fine details.
</Callout>

The combat system relies heavily on precise timing, dodging, and executing complex ability rotations. Configuring your input layout to match your personal preferences can dramatically improve your combat efficiency and reaction times during challenging boss encounters.

| Action | Keyboard & Mouse | Controller (Xbox / PlayStation) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Move Character | WASD | Left Thumbstick |
| Light Attack | Left Click | X / Square |
| Heavy Attack | Right Click | Y / Triangle |
| Dodge / Dash | Spacebar | B / Circle |
| Reincarnation Skill | Q | LB + RB / L1 + R1 |
| Interact | E | X / Square |

Keyboard and mouse setups allow for instant camera panning, which is highly beneficial when tracking multiple targets in open areas. However, the analog input of a controller provides superior movement precision, making it easier to navigate narrow platforms and dodge incoming area-of-effect attacks.

For players utilizing keyboard and mouse, remapping the dodge action to a side mouse button can free up your thumb for jumping and other movement controls. Additionally, binding your active skills to keys close to WASD (such as R, F, and C) ensures you can execute abilities without lifting your fingers off the movement keys.

<Callout type="info" title="Controller Support">

If you encounter crashes on startup, check if your system meets the minimum requirements. Many launch day crashes are caused by outdated operating system files or missing DirectX components. Running the game client as an administrator can also resolve permission conflicts with security software.

Another common issue is screen tearing, which occurs when the frame rate of the game does not align with your monitor refresh rate. Enabling V-Sync in the options menu or setting a frame rate cap via your GPU control panel can resolve this visual issue and provide a much smoother rendering experience.

<Callout type="warning" title="Overlay Conflicts">
Overlays from applications like Discord, GeForce Experience, and RivaTuner can sometimes cause stability issues, micro-stuttering, or input lag. If you experience unexpected performance drops, try disabling these overlays one by one to isolate the issue.
</Callout>
