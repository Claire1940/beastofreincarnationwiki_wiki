<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Performance Expectations on Steam Deck">

Running a modern Unreal Engine 5 title on portable hardware presents unique challenges. Unreal Engine 5 features advanced lighting and geometry pipelines that can heavily tax the Steam Deck custom APU. Handheld gamers should set realistic expectations regarding frame rates and visual fidelity.

Achieving a locked 60 FPS is highly unlikely without severe compromises to image quality. Instead, a targeted 30 FPS limit will provide a much smoother, more consistent gameplay experience. This prevents aggressive battery drain and keeps system temperatures within safe thresholds during extended play sessions.

| Graphic Setting | Recommended Value | Impact on Performance |
|---|---|---|
| Resolution | 1280x800 | Native display resolution, high impact |
| Shadows | Low to Medium | Reduces CPU overhead significantly |
| Textures | Medium | Fits within the Deck shared VRAM |
| Upscaling | FSR / TSR Enabled | Essential for stable frame rates |
| Frame Limit | 30 FPS | Prevents thermal throttling and stuttering |

<Callout type="warning" title="Unreal Engine 5 Stuttering">
Many modern UE5 releases suffer from shader compilation stutter. Keeping your SteamOS updated and using pre-compiled shader caches provided by Steam will be essential to minimize these performance drops.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Optimization Guide">

To ensure the best possible experience when launching the game for the first time, follow this step-by-step setup procedure. Tuning both your system settings and in-game configurations will maximize stability.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Update SteamOS and Proton">
    Navigate to your Steam Deck settings menu and check for system updates. Ensure you are running the latest stable version of SteamOS to receive the newest GPU driver optimizations.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Force Compatibility Tool">
    Go to the game properties in your Steam Library. Under the Compatibility tab, check the box to force the use of a specific tool, and select Proton Experimental or the latest Proton Hotfix release.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Set System Frame Limit">
    Open the Quick Access Menu by pressing the three-dot button on the right side of your Steam Deck. Under the Performance tab, set the frame rate limit to 30 FPS and match the refresh rate to 60Hz or 90Hz depending on your model.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Adjust In-Game Upscaling">
    Once in the game options menu, navigate to the video settings. Enable AMD FSR or Unreal Engine temporal super resolution (TSR) and set it to Balanced or Quality mode to boost rendering efficiency.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Compatibility Layer | Performance Stability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Proton Stable | High | Recommended for general compatibility |
| Proton Experimental | Moderate | Fixes specific launch day graphics bugs |
| Proton GE (Custom) | High | Often provides superior video playback compatibility |

<Callout type="tip" title="Battery Saving Tip">
Lowering the TDP (Thermal Design Power) limit to 10W or 11W in the Quick Access Menu can extend your battery life by up to 30 minutes while maintaining a stable 30 FPS.
</Callout>

</Section>
