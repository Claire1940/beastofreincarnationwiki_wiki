<Section icon={Map} title="Melee Mechanics and Katana Customization">

Emma's katana is her primary tool for survival. Rather than relying on ammunition or mana, melee combat is governed by the **Entanglement Gauge** at the bottom center of the screen. Striking enemies and executing perfect parries charges this gauge, allowing you to execute high-tier physical skills such as aerial downward strikes and vine-piercing summons.

To customize your playstyle, you can slot **Spirit Stones** directly into your equipment. These stones stack passive buffs whenever you perform a successful parry.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Vigor Build">
    Focuses on equipping Spirit Stones that grant the **Vigor buff** after parrying, boosting overall speed and physical attack power.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Elemental Hunter">
    Uses stones that modify ranged shots to track targets automatically while boosting damage against unalerted enemies.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Adrenaline Edge">
    A high-risk setup that significantly boosts your critical rate and raw attack power when Emma's HP drops below a specific threshold.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

Your katana's performance is heavily influenced by your armor's available slots. Emma's default Light Gambeson provides four slots for Spirit Stones, but advanced armors found throughout the world can hold up to six slots.

| Katana Stat | Impact on Gameplay | Recommended Modification |
|--- |--- |--- |
| **Attack** | Increases raw physical damage | Stack physical damage Spirit Stones |
| **Critical Rate** | Probability of dealing double damage | Combine with low-HP high-crit armor sets |
| **Skill Power** | Boosts the damage of vine-based skills | Equip stones that regenerate HP on hit |

<Callout type="warning" title="Parry Timing">
Parrying is not just defensive. Failing to parry drops your Entanglement build-up, preventing you from using Emma's crowd-control vine skills when surrounded by hostile Golems.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Ranged Arsenal and Elemental Ammunition">

While the katana handles close-range threats, your bow and crossbow are essential for managing crowds and exploiting elemental weaknesses. Emma can equip up to three active slots of specialized ammunition, allowing quick swaps mid-battle.

Your ranged arsenal relies on a variety of elemental affinities, which can be crafted or found as drops from corrupted Malefacts.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Equip Ammunition">
    Assign up to three different elemental arrows or crossbow bolts to your quick-access weapon slots.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Apply Status Ailments">
    Fire at distant targets to inflict status effects such as Burn or Poison before they close the distance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Trigger Blooming Arts Mods">
    Use Koo's customized Blooming Arts that deal bonus damage or grant Koo focus points (FP) when hitting afflicted targets.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Ammunition Type | Elemental Affinity | Best Used Against | Primary Effect |
|--- |--- |--- |--- |
| **Fire Arrow** | Fire | Plant-based Malefacts | Deals damage over time, triggers fire mods |
| **Poison Bolt** | Poison | High-HP Golems | Slowly drains health pools during long evasive phases |
| **Tracking Arrow** | Physical / Utility | Fast-moving aerial beasts | Automatically curves toward targets when fired |

<Callout type="info" title="Crossbow vs Bow">
The crossbow offers a much higher rate of fire, making it the superior choice for rapidly building up elemental status bars on bosses. The standard bow excels at long-range precision shots on unalerted targets.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Koo's Blooming Arts and Synergistic Skills">

You cannot master the **beast of reincarnation weapons** without utilizing Koo, your Malefact companion. While Emma is controlled directly, Koo is commanded via a tactical menu that slows down time. Selecting a Blooming Art triggers quick-time events (QTEs) that execute coordinated duo attacks.

Koo's abilities cost Focus Points (FP), which are displayed at the bottom right corner of the screen.

| Blooming Art | FP Cost | Target Range | Primary Combat Effect |
|--- |--- |--- |--- |
| **Flame Strike** | Low | Single Target | Deals fire damage, applies burn status |
| **Earth Restrain** | Medium | Area of Effect | Summons vines to group and immobilize up to four foes |
| **Aerial Launch** | Medium | Single Target | Launches enemies into the air for Emma's downward strike |

You can further customize Koo's Blooming Arts by attaching specialized **mods** that alter their behavior based on Emma's active weapon state.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Weapon Progression and Boss Rewards">

Upgrading your weapons and expanding your tactical options requires defeating the giant bosses of the blight, known as Nushi. Defeating a Nushi rewards Emma with unique flowers that sprout in her hair, unlocking new skills and marking your progression through the game's timeline.

| Boss Encounter | Reward Unlocked | Combat Application |
|--- |--- |--- |
| **Carnivorous Plant Nushi** | Shakuyaku Flower | Unlocks resurrection mechanic and initial vine skills |
| **Deer Nushi** | Sakura Flower / Purple Tendrils | Enhances melee range and adds tracking to ranged shots |
| **Bear Nushi** | Eleven-Flower Mastery | Unlocks high-tier skill tree paths for both Emma and Koo |

<Callout type="info" title="Equipment Customization">
Koo's attributes can also be modified using collectible charms. These charms alter his defensive capabilities and his FP regeneration rates during intense boss encounters.
